Yet another - Wikipedia Yet another From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia (Redirected from Yet Another) Jump to navigation Jump to search Idiomatic qualifier used in the name of programs Among programmers, yet another (often abbreviated ya, Ya, or YA in the initial part of an acronym) is an idiomatic qualifier in the name of a computer program, organisation, or event that is confessedly unoriginal.[1] Stephen C. Johnson is credited with establishing the naming convention in the late 1970s when he named his compiler-compiler yacc (Yet Another Compiler-Compiler), since he felt there were already numerous compiler-compilers in circulation at the time.
