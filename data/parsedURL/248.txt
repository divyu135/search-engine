Find sources: "Field specification" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR (December 2009) (Learn how and when to remove this template message) A field specification or fspec defines a portion of a word in some programming language. It has the form "(L:R)" where "L" is the leftmost byte and "R" is the rightmost byte, and counting begins at zero. For example, the first three bytes of a word would have the fspec (0:2) for bytes numbered 0, 1, and 2.
