Lynx (programming language) - Wikipedia Lynx (programming language) From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Jump to navigation Jump to search Lynx is a programming language for large distributed networks, using remote procedure calls. It was developed by the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1984 for the Charlotte multicomputer operating system. In 1986 at the University of Rochester Lynx was ported to the Chrysalis operating system running on a BBN Butterfly multiprocessor. Bibliography[edit] M. L.
